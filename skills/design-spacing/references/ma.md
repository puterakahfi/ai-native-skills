# Ma (間) — Intentional Space

> Ma is a Japanese concept: the space between things is not empty — it has presence.
> Applied to UI: purposeful emptiness that gives content room to breathe and speak.

## Ma vs Dead Space

```
MA (good):                      DEAD SPACE (bad):
  Space that creates calm          Space that creates confusion
  Silence between notes            Note that was forgotten
  White space with intent          White space from absence
  Content needs room to land       Content feels lost and adrift
  1–2 focal points per viewport    No focal point at all

Test: remove the space — does content feel crowded?
  Yes → Ma was working (keep it)
  No  → Dead space (fill or restructure)
```

## Ma by Genre

```
ZEN/MINIMALIST (maximum Ma):
  Section spacing: clamp(120px, 18vh, 200px)
  Hero padding-top: clamp(140px, 20vh, 220px)
  One focal object per viewport — eye rests, then moves
  Principle: Seijaku — silence is the design

EDITORIAL (generous Ma):
  Section spacing: clamp(80px, 12vh, 140px)
  Wide margins — content column max 70ch
  Space = authority, not emptiness

MODERN MINIMAL/SAAS (efficient Ma):
  Section spacing: clamp(64px, 8vh, 96px)
  Denser — information density is part of the product
  Ma applied selectively to hero + key moments

ATMOSPHERIC (dramatic Ma):
  Section spacing: clamp(100px, 16vh, 180px)
  Vast empty darks — space IS the atmosphere
  Content emerges from space, not sits in it
```

## Ma Gates (review checklist)

```
□ Hero has breathing room before first scroll? (padding-top ≥ 100px)
□ Each section has distinct entry space? (margin-top ≥ 60px)
□ No two major focal points compete in same viewport?
□ Sparse content sections don't feel abandoned?
  → Fix: reduce spacing, not increase — Ma ≠ padding around nothing
□ White space consistent temperature with palette?
  → warm bg + warm white space ✓ / warm bg + stark white space ✗
```
