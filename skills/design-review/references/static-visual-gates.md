# Static Visual Gates

Load this reference for posters, flyers, banners, social creatives, advertisements, thumbnails, presentation slides, exported graphics, and print-oriented visual artifacts.

Use it together with `universal-gates.md`. Interactive gates are `NOT_APPLICABLE` unless the artifact contains an interactive product mockup being reviewed as a secondary surface.

## Required context

Before scoring, record:

```text
artifact type
intended channel or placement
final dimensions and aspect ratio
expected viewing distance or on-screen size
crop and safe-area constraints
print or digital output
required text, offer, price, contact, logo, product, face, and legal content
reference assets that must remain faithful
```

Do not judge only from a zoomed editor canvas.

## Message and attention

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| SV1 Primary Message | Can the viewer identify the dominant message, offer, or subject within the expected attention window? | Actual-size inspection shows one clear entry point and understandable supporting sequence. |
| SV2 Attention Flow | Does the eye move through headline, subject/product, proof/detail, and action in the intended order? | Scale, placement, contrast, direction, and whitespace create a coherent path. |
| SV3 Information Priority | Are mandatory details prioritized according to audience need rather than given equal weight? | Price, date, contact, CTA, specification, or disclaimer are neither lost nor competing with the main message. |
| SV4 Channel Fit | Does the message density and visual strategy suit the intended feed, story, banner, thumbnail, screen, room, or print context? | The artifact remains understandable in its real placement. |

## Legibility and viewing conditions

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| SV5 Actual-Size Legibility | Is critical text readable at final output size and expected distance? | Tested at 100% export size or realistic device/print simulation. |
| SV6 Text Survival | Do small labels, prices, contacts, disclaimers, and secondary copy survive compression, scaling, and export? | No thin, low-contrast, tightly tracked, or undersized required text disappears. |
| SV7 Background Interference | Does imagery, texture, glow, shadow, or gradient preserve text and subject clarity? | Critical content remains stable across the full text area, not only one favorable spot. |

A large source canvas does not prove legibility. Review thumbnails as thumbnails, stories behind platform overlays, and posters from the intended physical distance.

## Asset and brand fidelity

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| SV8 Brand Fidelity | Are logo construction, clear space, color, typography, and required brand cues accurate? | Compared with supplied brand assets or declared brand rules. |
| SV9 Product Fidelity | Are product shape, packaging, color, labels, ports, controls, and distinguishing details preserved? | Compared directly with the provided product reference. |
| SV10 Human Fidelity | When a specific person or talent must be preserved, are identity, facial structure, skin tone, clothing, pose constraints, and required attributes accurate? | Compared with the approved reference; stylization is declared and controlled. |
| SV11 Content Accuracy | Are names, prices, dates, contact details, specifications, claims, and legal text correct? | Verified against user-provided or authoritative source content. |

For commercial or identity-critical work, a material failure in SV8, SV9, SV10, or SV11 is a hard gate. Visual polish cannot compensate for an altered logo, wrong product, changed face, incorrect price, or fabricated claim.

## Composition and format safety

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| SV12 Crop Safety | Does essential content survive the required crop and responsive placement variants? | Checked against actual aspect ratios and crop boundaries. |
| SV13 Safe Areas and Overlays | Are critical elements clear of story UI, feed controls, notches, player controls, trimming, and platform overlays? | Safe-area template or placement simulation confirms clearance. |
| SV14 Bleed and Trim | For print, are bleed, trim, margins, and edge-sensitive content handled correctly? | Print specification evidence exists. |
| SV15 Subject Separation | Is the product, person, or focal visual clearly separated without looking artificially cut out or detached? | Edge quality, grounding, shadow, lighting, and depth are coherent. |
| SV16 Balance at Final Ratio | Does visual weight remain intentional after final crop and export? | Actual-ratio inspection shows no accidental empty corner, edge crowding, or competing mass. |

## Image and production quality

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| SV17 Resolution | Does the export have sufficient effective resolution for the intended use? | Pixel dimensions or print DPI match delivery requirements. |
| SV18 Edge and Mask Quality | Are cutouts, hair, transparent edges, shadows, and compositing clean at delivery size? | No halos, jagged edges, missing details, or inconsistent blur. |
| SV19 Lighting and Perspective | Do combined assets share believable light direction, scale, camera angle, and perspective when realism is intended? | Subject and environment feel physically coherent. |
| SV20 Compression and Color | Does export preserve sharpness, gradients, color, and contrast in the destination format? | Final file, not only the editor preview, is inspected. |
| SV21 Generative Artifact Control | Is the design free from distorted text, malformed hands/objects, duplicate details, impossible geometry, and accidental marks? | Full-frame and detail inspection completed. |

## Presentation-specific gates

Apply only when the profile is `presentation`.

| Gate | Review question | Pass evidence |
|---|---|---|
| PR1 One Dominant Idea | Does each slide communicate one primary claim, decision, or transition? | Supporting elements reinforce the slide title instead of forming a document page. |
| PR2 Narrative Continuity | Does the sequence build a coherent argument or story? | Adjacent slides have clear progression and no unexplained jumps. |
| PR3 Data Clarity | Are charts, diagrams, tables, and numbers readable and interpreted rather than merely displayed? | Key takeaway is visually encoded and sources are present where required. |
| PR4 Deck Consistency | Are repeated titles, margins, page numbers, labels, charts, and section transitions coherent? | Consistency supports navigation without making every slide identical. |
| PR5 Delivery Context | Does the slide work at room distance, screen share, or self-guided reading as declared? | Tested in the actual delivery mode. |

## Static quick review

After the universal quick review, score the applicable subset:

```text
SV1  primary message
SV2  attention flow
SV5  actual-size legibility
SV8  brand fidelity
SV9  product fidelity when applicable
SV10 human fidelity when applicable
SV11 content accuracy
SV12 crop safety
SV13 safe areas
SV17 resolution
SV21 generative artifact control
```

## Hard-gate policy

```text
Wrong required logo, product, person, price, date, contact, or claim → automatic fail for commercial delivery
Unreadable mandatory information at final size                    → automatic fail
Essential content outside required crop/safe area                  → automatic fail
Insufficient export resolution for declared delivery               → automatic fail
```

Mark a fidelity gate `NOT_APPLICABLE` only when no specific asset or fact was required. Do not mark it passed merely because the result looks plausible.
