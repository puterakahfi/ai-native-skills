# Color Psychology

> Emotional + cultural associations. Use to validate palette, not to pick it.
> Pick palette from genre + harmony first, then validate against psychology.

## Core Emotional Associations

```
RED       Energy, urgency, passion, danger, appetite
          Use: alerts, errors, food, sale/discount, CTA (sparingly)
          Avoid: trust-first products, healthcare (clinical context)

ORANGE    Warmth, creativity, enthusiasm, approachability
          Use: consumer, friendly SaaS, food, CTA buttons
          Avoid: luxury, zen, ultra-minimal (too loud)

YELLOW    Optimism, attention, caution, warmth
          Use: highlights, warnings, playful accents
          Avoid: text on white (contrast fails), luxury

GREEN     Growth, nature, health, success, go/safe
          Sage variant: zen, calm, natural → #7a9e7a
          Bright variant: success state, live status, CTAs
          Avoid: luxury (too common), tech (overused)

BLUE      Trust, calm, stability, professionalism, technology
          Use: SaaS, enterprise, finance, healthcare
          Avoid: food (appetite suppressor), playful (too corporate)

VIOLET    Creativity, mystery, luxury, spirituality
          Use: creative tools, AI products, premium SaaS
          Avoid: enterprise B2B (too abstract), food

BLACK     Elegance, power, sophistication, luxury
          Warm black: zen, editorial, premium
          Cold black: tech, atmospheric, modern minimal

WHITE     Purity, clarity, space, minimalism
          Warm white: editorial, zen, organic
          Pure white: modern minimal, Kanso (zen light mode)
```

## Cultural Caveats

```
WHITE:  mourning in East Asian cultures (JP, CN, KR) — context matters
RED:    luck + celebration in China; danger in Western contexts
GREEN:  sacred in some Islamic cultures
PURPLE: royalty in Western; mourning in Thailand
```

## Temperature Psychology

```
WARM (red/orange/yellow hues):
  Feels: close, inviting, energetic, organic
  Use:   zen (wabi-sabi), editorial, playful, food, lifestyle

COOL (blue/green/violet hues):
  Feels: distant, calm, professional, technological
  Use:   SaaS, enterprise, atmospheric, healthcare, finance

NEUTRAL (gray, black, white):
  Feels: sophisticated, restrained, timeless
  Use:   luxury, zen, editorial, modern minimal
```

## Validation Checklist

```
□ Palette temperature matches genre emotional target?
□ Accent color psychology aligns with product purpose?
□ No cultural conflicts for target audience region?
□ Saturation level matches genre energy (zen=low, atmospheric=high)?
□ Warm/cool consistency across all palette steps?
```
