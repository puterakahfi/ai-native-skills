## Experiment specification

### Target users
A curated cohort of existing customers, micro-creators, freelancers, educators, or small agencies whose audiences overlap with the product's users.

### Riskiest assumption
The **riskiest assumption** is that qualified partners will create and publish useful promotional content when the commission from a one-time SaaS purchase may be relatively small.

### Falsifiable hypothesis
This hypothesis is **falsifiable**: if qualified partners receive a clear offer, referral identity, disclosure rules, and a lightweight content kit, then a measurable share will publish original content and generate valid, activated customers during a fixed pilot period.

### Smallest test
Run a 30-day **manual pilot** with approximately 15 curated partners. Use manual approval, unique links or coupon codes, a lightweight ledger, manual reversal review, and weekly evidence capture.

### What will not be built
**What will not be built** during this test:
- a full self-service dashboard;
- automated payout integration;
- multi-level referrals;
- an open affiliate marketplace;
- lifetime or recurring commission logic;
- advanced campaign automation.

### Pre-registered decision rules

**PASS**
- at least 60% of approved partners publish an eligible content item within seven days;
- at least three different partners generate valid transactions;
- at least ten valid transactions occur;
- at least 70% of referred customers create a meaningful first output within seven days;
- direct commission and operating cost remain within the approved economics ceiling.

**PARTIAL**
- content publication or transactions occur, but results are concentrated in one or two partners;
- meaningful traffic appears but conversion or customer activation remains weak;
- unit economics are plausible but not yet stable.

**FAIL**
- fewer than 30% of approved partners publish;
- no valid transaction occurs;
- repeated observed behavior shows the incentive is not worth the effort;
- fraud, refund, support, or dispute cost makes the channel unsafe.

### Guardrail criteria
- **Guardrail:** no unsupported income claims, hidden affiliate relationship, spam, self-referral, attribution manipulation, or unnecessary customer-data exposure.
- Refunds, chargebacks, fraud, and disputes must remain below pre-approved thresholds.

### Evidence collection
Capture **evidence** from publication links, referral events, valid transactions, activation events, reversals, direct variable costs, partner interviews about actual behavior, and customer first-value behavior.

### Decision handoff
- PASS routes to product requirements and a bounded MVP slice.
- PARTIAL routes to one narrower follow-up experiment with a single changed assumption.
- FAIL stops platform development and evaluates a different channel model.
